Seung-Bin (Jake) Joo

I'm a MEng Engineering Science student at the University of Oxford, where I specialize in artificial intelligence, robotics, and computer vision.

I am currently completing my Master's Thesis on robot learning at the Visual Geometry Group, supervised by Dr. João Henriques. Before this, I worked on belief dynamics learning for blind robotic manipulation at the GOALS lab in the Oxford Robotics Institute, under the supervision of Prof. Nick Hawes.

Additionally, I've worked at the UCL Electrochemical Innovation Lab with Prof. James Robinson and Prof. Rhodri Jervis to apply deep learning and unsupervised learning to battery diagnostic acoustic techniques. I was also an intern at the Seoul National University Biorobotics Lab, developing pneumatic and control systems for dynamic cushion soft robots.

Research and Projects

I currently have broad interests in areas of deep learning and robot learning.

Language-conditioned and generalisable robotic manipulation with reinforcement learning
Seung-Bin Joo, João F. Henriques,
Master's Thesis (in progress), 2025
project page / paper

For real-life deployment, robots must adapt and generalize to diverse task configurations. It is also desired to imbue vision-language understanding into robot policies. We take inspiration from RMA and RL-VLM-F to tackle generalisable and language-conditioned bimanual robot manipulation.

Belief Dynamics Learning for Contact-Rich Robotic Manipulation via Differentiable Particle Filters
Seung-Bin Joo, Lara Brudermüller, Nick Hawes
EPSRC research, 2024
project page / poster

Belief dynamics learning for blind robotic grasping where we only have sparse contact observations. We use differentiable particle filters with a novel negative proposing pipeline.

Investigating the Performance and Safety of Li-Ion Cylindrical Cells Using Acoustic Emission and Machine Learning Analysis
Arthur Fordham, Seung-Bin Joo, Rhodri E. Owen, Elias Galiounas, Mark Buckwell, Dan J. L. Brett, Paul R. Shearing, Rhodri Jervis, James B. Robinson
Journal of the Electrochemical Society, 2024
project page / paper / poster

We propose acoustic emission (AE) analysis and deep neural networks to monitor state-of-health of cylindrical lithium-ion batteries, and unsupervised machine learning methods to determine the degradation phenomena behind AE signals.
